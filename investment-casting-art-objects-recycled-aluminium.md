My final year project (under Dr. S.R. Reddy, Department of Metallurgical & Materials Engineering, VNIT Nagpur), running July 2025 – May 2026.

## The problem

Investment casting of intricate art objects usually depends on hand-tooled or injection-molded wax patterns — expensive and slow for one-off or low-volume geometries. This project develops a **hybrid pattern-making route** that combines FDM 3D printing, liquid silicone rubber (LSR) mold replication, and conventional wax casting, so intricate geometries can be produced without a full tooling investment.

## Approach

- FDM-printed masters feed an LSR replication step, producing wax patterns for the shell-and-burnout investment casting process.
- Burnout and casting parameters are optimized for the hybrid route specifically, rather than reused from conventional wax-pattern casting.
- **Flux-treated recycled aluminium** is used as the casting alloy, poured under **vacuum assistance** to minimize porosity and inclusion-related defects that recycled feedstock is prone to.
- Casting quality is evaluated through microstructural characterization (SEM, optical microscopy) and hardness testing.

## Why it matters

This sits at the intersection of two things I care about: using additive manufacturing as a **front-end to traditional metal-forming processes** rather than a replacement for them, and treating "recycled material is inferior" as a process-control problem to be solved rather than an inherent limitation.
